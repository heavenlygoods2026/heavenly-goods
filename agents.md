# Heavenly Goods - Agentic Governance Framework (agents.md)

## Document Purpose
This document serves as the comprehensive agentic governance framework for the Heavenly Goods e-commerce platform. Building upon the "antigravity" base template, it dictates the strict technical architecture, visual consistency, and data synchronization standards required for the transition from the legacy Wix platform. Furthermore, it functions as a living friction and regression log to prevent the reintroduction of technical debt.

## 1. Technical Architecture & Tech Stack
To ensure high performance and seamless real-time data synchronization across the e-commerce ecosystem, all AI agents and developers must adhere to the following stack constraints:

*   **Core Framework**: Strict TypeScript and React 19.
*   **Styling**: Tailwind CSS 4.0 is mandatory for utility-first styling. The use of legacy CSS modules is explicitly forbidden to maintain absolute visual consistency.
*   **Backend & Data Sync**: Firebase and Cloudflare R2 must be leveraged for real-time synchronization of inventory data, user profiles, and high-resolution product imagery.
*   **Future-Proofing**: The web architecture must be built with a bridge-first mindset to facilitate a planned future transition to a native SwiftUI mobile application.

## 2. Brand Identity & Visual Consistency
Agents must rigorously enforce the Heavenly Goods brand aesthetic across all UI components. The brand is fundamentally faith-based; the core mission is to celebrate God’s love and remind customers of their identity in Christ through every detail. Shopping here is about sharing a message of faith, love, and purpose, not just buying cute items.

### Design Directives
*   **Color Palette**: UI colors must reflect the physical packaging of the products: predominantly soft pastel pinks, complemented by vibrant oranges, warm taupes, and metallic gold accents.
*   **Textures & Motifs**: Digital elements should evoke the tactile, delicate "handmade" nature of the brand. Visual cues can draw inspiration from the textured crinkle paper and clear protective packaging used in shipments.
*   **Typography**: The platform should incorporate elements that mirror the delicate "handmade - made with love -" cursive script and minimalist flower icons found on the physical product backing cards.

## 3. Product Data Ecosystem & Localization
The database architecture must strictly support bilingual functionality (English and Spanish) to accommodate diverse product listings. Current inventory synchronization must account for the following categories and pricing structures:

*   **Hair Accessories**: e.g., "Crowned in Grace: Hair Bows & Clips" ($11.99), characterized by layered ribbons, rhinestones, pastel flowers, and metallic heart embellishments.
*   **Custom & Pre-made Bracelets**: Includes the "Create Your Own Bracelet" ($4.99) requiring dynamic user input fields, alongside handmade "I Love Jesus" bracelets for adults ($4.99) and kids ($2.99).
*   **Bilingual Keychains**: Essential product lines requiring English/Spanish localized data fields, such as the "Personalized Letter Resin Keychain" (Llavero Resin con Letra Personalizada) ($3.99), the "2 Timothy 1:7 Keychain Collection" ($7.99), and the "2 Corinthians 5:17 Keychain Collection" ($3.99).
